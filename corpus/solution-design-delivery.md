category: Solution Design & Delivery

Alex's delivery method comes from both sides of the table: consulting engagements where he ran client workshops, and platform ownership where he lives with the result.

**Discovery that closes ambiguity.** Run technical discovery sessions that turn vague requirements into concrete artefacts: user stories, acceptance criteria, test cases, and a process map. Play the design back to stakeholders before building, so misunderstandings surface on a whiteboard, not in production.

**Decisions in writing, with attribution.** Every non-trivial decision lands in a working note with the name and date of who decided it. Verbal agreements evaporate; an attributed written record is what you stand on three months later when someone asks why.

**Design documents that earn their length.** Front-load the recommendation, then the evidence. Use tables for current-state findings, severity, and file/record citations. Include an explicit "what I'm not covering" section so scope is unambiguous.

**Review external designs against the real system.** When a vendor or third party submits a high-level design, check it against what's actually deployed — the records, fields, and constraints that exist — not against the design's own narrative. The biggest issues are usually the assumptions the document never states.

**Phase around what you don't control.** If a deliverable depends on another team's contract or decision, build Phase 1 now behind a stub and name Phase 2 as the live integration. Keep momentum without pretending the dependency is resolved.

**Own the outcome.** The frame for every recommendation is the person who has to support it after go-live — favour the option that fails safe and is easy to reason about under pressure.
